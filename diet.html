Diet

A common saying is that you get abs in the kitchen, not in the gym. Your diet plays an imporant role to your fitness journey; without proper care, results will never show. Although we are not dietitian, we have basic knowledge that will bring you to the right path.

Your diet should be centred on giving your body enough calories and nutrients to promote muscle growth and repair if you're wanting to put on muscle. Protein is crucial for repairing and growing muscle. Aim to eat 1 gram of protein per pound of body weight per day or more. Lean meats, poultry, fish, eggs, dairy products, beans, and legumes are all excellent sources of protein.

Staying hydrated is crucial for any fitness activities as it allows for improved preformance. Studies have shown that 75% of adults in the U.s are dehydrated. Make sure you drink enough water, 3L a day.







Calorie Intake

The quantity of energy received from food and beverages is known as calorie intake, a term that many individuals find terrifying. More calories consumed results in weight increase, whereas fewer calories consumed results in weight decrease. Having a balanced calorie intake can lead to a good health. The age, gender, height, weight, and amount of exercises the person does all affect it differently.
